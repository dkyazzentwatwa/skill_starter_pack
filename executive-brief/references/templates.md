# Executive Brief Templates

Use these templates as defaults. Adapt section density to the situation, but preserve the core logic.

## Standard Brief

### Title
One-line description of the situation and why it matters.

### Executive Summary
- 3 to 6 bullets only
- lead with the most important conclusion, risk, or decision
- include one bullet if the inputs materially conflict

### Current Situation
- 3 to 6 bullets describing the state of play in plain language
- include the most relevant recent change

### Key Facts
Group facts by theme when helpful.

For each important bullet, prefer one of these labels:
- **Confirmed:**
- **Inferred:**
- **Missing / Unverified:**

### Open Questions
List only questions that materially affect timing, ownership, decision quality, or risk.

### Decisions Needed
For each decision, include:
- what must be decided
- by when, if known
- what happens if the decision slips

### Risks / Blockers
Separate active blockers from emerging risks when possible.

### Recommended Next Steps
Keep these concrete and close to the evidence.

### Owner Suggestions
Suggest owners only when reasonably supported by the inputs or role context.
If ownership is unclear, say so.

### Appendix / Source Notes
Use short source tags such as:
- meeting notes
- email thread
- voice transcript excerpt
- launch doc
- finance update
- slack recap

Note conflicts or weak evidence here if they do not belong in the executive summary.

## Rapid Brief

Use for urgent, partial, or fast-turnaround requests.

### Title
### Executive Summary
- 3 to 5 bullets

### What Matters Now
- immediate situation only

### Decisions / Asks
- explicit asks first
- inferred asks second, clearly labeled

### Risks / Blockers
- only the highest-signal items

### Immediate Next Steps
- near-term actions only

### Source Notes
- list main sources and any major caveats

## Deep Brief

Use for complex, multi-source, or high-stakes situations.

### Title
### Executive Summary
### Current Situation
### Key Facts by Theme
Suggested themes:
- timeline
- commercial impact
- product / ops status
- stakeholder alignment
- staffing / resourcing
- financial exposure

### Timeline / Deadlines
Use explicit dates where available.

### Stakeholder Map
List the parties involved and their likely role or stance when supported by the inputs.

### Open Questions
### Decisions Needed
Where useful, present options with tradeoffs.

### Risks / Blockers
### Recommended Next Steps
### Owner Suggestions
### Appendix / Source Notes
Include:
- source mapping
- known conflicts
- assumptions
- confidence-limiting gaps

## Labeling Pattern

Use labels where they add clarity, especially in Key Facts, Open Questions, Risks / Blockers, and Appendix / Source Notes.

Examples:
- **Confirmed:** Vendor contract redlines were still open as of March 11.
- **Inferred:** The launch date is likely to slip if legal review is not completed this week.
- **Missing / Unverified:** No confirmed owner was named for partner onboarding.

## Decision Framing Pattern

Use this structure when a decision needs crisp framing:

- **Decision:** what must be decided
- **Why it matters:** operational or strategic consequence
- **Deadline:** explicit date or timing window
- **If delayed:** likely downside
- **Input quality:** confirmed / inferred / mixed
