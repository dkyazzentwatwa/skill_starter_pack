# Executive Brief Review Checklist

Use this checklist before finalizing the brief.

## Evidence discipline
- Can each major claim be traced to the provided inputs?
- Are Confirmed, Inferred, and Missing / Unverified used correctly?
- Did any sentence accidentally turn a guess into a fact?

## Signal quality
- Does the executive summary contain only the highest-signal points?
- Are decisions, blockers, and deadlines easy to spot?
- Did low-value recap or transcript noise get cut?

## Conflict handling
- Are contradictions surfaced rather than hidden?
- Did the brief avoid false certainty?
- If inputs conflict, is the unresolved part still visible?

## Recommendation quality
- Do next steps follow from the evidence?
- Are owner suggestions grounded rather than random?
- Is each recommendation specific enough to act on?

## Brevity and usefulness
- Is the brief materially shorter and sharper than the source material?
- Are long paragraphs replaced with bullets where possible?
- Could a busy founder grasp the situation fast?

## Final pass
- Remove duplicates.
- Tighten wording.
- Make dates explicit.
- Keep tone neutral and operator-focused.
- Ensure the output is ready for human review before any external-facing use.
